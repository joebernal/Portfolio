About Me

Hi, I’m Joe Bernal, a Junior studying Computer Science at California State University, Fullerton. My journey into tech has been shaped by more than just curiosity — it's been forged through resilience, faith, and a desire to serve others.

After surviving cancer, I came out with a renewed purpose: to build technology that makes a difference in people’s lives. Whether it’s helping students graduate on time or making healthcare information more accessible, I want to create tools that serve the overlooked and underserved.

I’ve built full-stack web applications using Python, Flask, SQL, and Google Cloud. Some of my projects include a Course Recommendation System for Citrus College CS students, a Global Travel Database that helps users explore destinations by safety and affordability, and an interactive Campus Navigation System.

Outside of tech, I lead a Bible study group and teach salsa dancing. I also had the life-changing opportunity to study abroad in Barcelona, where I immersed myself in Spanish culture, built friendships across Europe and Latin America, and grew spiritually and personally.

I believe great engineers are not just coders; they’re communicators, teachers, and learners. I’m always looking to grow, collaborate, and contribute to meaningful, mission-driven teams.
